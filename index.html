Beyond GDP.

Accounting for externalities. Both negative and positive.

For example, regenerative agriculture:

  • uses less fertiliser...
  • ...means less agricultural runoff that goes to the ocean...
  • ...means less algae bloom, dead zones, acidification
  • livestock is eating grass and their poo acts as a fertiliser
  • enriches the quality of the soil
  • less fuel used for heavy machinery
  • creating local jobs
  • educating about food system
  • building resilient communities
  • protecting biodiversity and pollinators (no bees = no good)

In the current system - someone else get subsidised fertilizer - so how do you stay in business?
